App Inventor and its Distributions | XYBERNEO

Aditya Chaturvedi July 1, 2021 AI2

Describe App Inventor in one line?
MIT App Inventor is an online platform to learn/build apps without coding! 

App Inventor is a web application environment originally developed by Google, and now maintained by Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)

App Inventor makes developing apps easier without a single line of code, it allows users to create applications for Android & IOS (currently in final beta testing)

Any users a freelance developer to a full developer whoever you are, you can try hands-on App Inventor. You can create apps, learn by the tutorials, find a resource for your school. 

App Inventor also has an active community, where you can ask queries related to AI2. If you are a developer you can contribute to App Inventor development via our open-source repository on GitHub.

App Inventor is free and open-source software released under dual licensing: a Creative Commons Attribution ShareAlike 3.0 Unported license, and an Apache License 2.0 for the source code.

Here’s a quick guide to get started with App Inventor.

1. Open your browser and enter the URL appinventor.mit.edu or click here.

2. Then click on the Create Apps Button which will head over to the Login Page,

Login in with any existing mail id.

3. After the successful login, you will have to accept the TOS which will take you to the main page.

4. Where a Welcome Pop-up will appear. To create apps click on Start New Project a pop-up will appear where you can enter the app/project name.

Hurray…..you successfully created your first project

Now as your project is created, let's see how to use App Inventor. When your Project is Opened, you will see an Interface for creating apps called Designer. Where it has Component Platte at Left an Android Device Mockup at Center and a Properties tab at Right along with some Buttons in the top menu.

Now you can use the components from Palette and drag them to Mockup Screen to add then in your app and use Properties tab to change look/properties of the component. You can also add External Files like Images, Fonts, Pdf, Json, Etc. By Clicking on the Upload Button below the Components tab.

Now, by clicking on the Blocks button we will go the to blocks Section i.e the logical part of your App, where you will use blocks to perform Action on Event, Execute Procedures and more. 

As mentioned before App Inventor is an Open Source software, so you can make your own app inventor.

For personal use, it can be hosted on your PC, or hosted on a cloud platform as App Inventor is.

App Inventor's Code and related Information can be found in their Github Repository



There are many App Inventor Clone also known as App Inventor Distributions. App Inventor distributions are same as AI2 but with different themes & tweaks. They are kind of commercial version so you will see may differents components in then except that one in AI2. The extra components are very useful. You can also monetize your application by using ads components which are present there and many more...



As these distros provide you many different things along with the basic app inventor, you will have to pay them. Most of them have a monthly/early subscription. But some works on different model get paid. For Example. Kodular is completely free of cost, you will have to pay $0 to use it. But if you want to monetize your app, then kodular will take some part of ads and display their ads as a part of commission based revenue system. This commission can be removed by paying a monthly fee.

Similarly Niotron has a different type of model to generate revenue from their service. This builder is not recommended for people who want a dedicated support team and fellow users to interact with in the community. Moreover, there is no way you can monetize your app without paying for it

Some of the known Distros are :

1. Appy Builder (Was merged with kodular)

2. Thunkable

3. Kodular

4. Block2Code (Closed)

5. Casagbic (Temporarily down for update)

6. Niotron

7. Apppro (Shut down due to lack of funds)

8. WxBit Chinese version

9. Appzard 

And Many more... 

Based on the title of the topic we have asked some interesting questions to some of the known personalities in MIT AI2 and its Distributions.

The answers from them are pretty Intresting :)

+ What are your views on the recent increase in the app inventor clones and their future ? 

Answer by Pavitra Golchha 

As MIT App Inventor is open source, anyone is free to modify and redistribute it, like Kodular did. The recent increase in the clones shows that they want to extend the platform which couldn't made possible with App Inventor itself due to its use of education. And this is good because they spread the message of App Inventor which is enabling everyone to create apps.

Pavitra Golchha, VP of Innovation Kodular

+ Your views on IOS App Development with AI2 ?

Answer by Evan Patton  

We continue to work with Apple App Store Review to get the MIT App Inventor Companion approved for use on iOS. They have more stringent requirements than Google so it's taking more time than I think most folks would like, but we have to play by their rules. We have a couple thousand beta testers using it at this point and it seems to be going fairly well. Internally we're also turning our attention toward the build system and what that will entail from an infrastructure perspective.

Evan Patton, lead software engineer for the MIT App Inventor

+ How do you see App Inventor and it’s distributions like kodular in next 5 years ?

Answer by Taifun 

MIT App Inventor still will be there, there is MIT behind and as it is open source it will live “forever”…

distributions usually are closed source, so it will depend on the teams… an important part is being able to pay the bills, for example server costs as well as improving the builder, so there must be something to generate income… without that and without a large user base there will be no future for a distribution

Taifun, PuraVida Apps

+ Can AI2 completely replace Android Studio in future ?

Answer by Peter Mathijssen

In theory it could, but i don't think that will happen. What App Inventor also tries to do is to get children interested in programming. Chances are they will switch to other languages after they reach the current limitations of App Inventor.

Peter Mathijssen, Moderator AI2/Kodular

+ Some tips or suggestions to new young students/users using this platform.

Answer by Evan Patton

A important piece of advice I'd give is that through App Inventor you have a great opportunity to use apps to shape the world around you. We've seem some fantastic apps over the years from both young students and adults who have little to no coding experience but see a struggle of a friend or family member and make an impact in their lives. That is an extremely powerful gift. So as you use App Inventor, think about the challenges you see in the world and in your communities and think about how the skills you are learning could be put to use to improve someone's life or the lives of many.

Evan Patton, lead software engineer for the MIT App Inventor
